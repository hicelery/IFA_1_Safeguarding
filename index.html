What is Safeguarding?

Safeguarding means protecting people from abuse, neglect and harm.

Key elements

Prevention: Safe recruitment, training, risk assessments and creating safe environments.
Recognition: Understanding indicators of physical, emotional, financial or sexual abuse and neglect.
Response: Clear reporting routes, timely investigations and proportionate action to protect people.
Empowerment: Involving individuals in decisions about their care and promoting independence.
Confidentiality: Sharing information only when necessary to protect someone and keeping accurate records.

Safeguarding is for everyone: it's everyone's responsibility to ensure that individuals, especially those who are vulnerable, are safe and their well-being is promoted.

Signs to Watch

Withdrawal

Sudden changes in behaviour, mood swings, or withdrawal from social activities.

Behavioral Changes

Changes in eating or sleeping patterns, increased anxiety, or fear of certain situations.

Physical Signs

Unexplained injuries, bruising, changes in appearance, or signs of neglect.

Fearfulness

Being unusually fearful or anxious around specific individuals or situations.

Look for patterns and trust your instincts if something feels off.

Action Steps
  • 1. Ensure immediate safety. In an emergency, call 999 or your local emergency number immediately.
  • 2. Report concerns
  • 3. Record observations
  • 4. Follow up
